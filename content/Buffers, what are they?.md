---
title: Buffers, what are they?
publish: "true"
---
 A buffer is a temporary storage area in memory that holds data while it's being moved from one place to another.

  Think of it like a loading dock at a warehouse - stuff sits there temporarily before being moved to its final destination.

  ---
  The Core Concept

  Buffer = Temporary Waiting Area for Data

  Source  →  BUFFER  →  Destination

  Data flows through the buffer instead of going directly.
